The Great Hymn to the Aten

You rise in beauty on the horizon of the sky,
O Living Aten, source of all life.

When you ascend in the eastern light,
You fill every land with your radiance.
Your brilliance reaches to the farthest corners —
Even the unknown lands receive your light.

Though you are high above,
Your rays touch the earth below.
Though your form is hidden,
Your presence is known through what you sustain.

The world is brought to life by your hand.
Plants grow, trees blossom, birds sing,
Their wings outstretched in praise of your spirit.

O Aten, Living Sun,
You are eternal, you are one.
The earth rejoices in your light,
And I rejoice in you.

Aten was originally just the physical sun disk, but during the reign of Akhenaten, Aten was elevated to the one true god in a brief experiment with monotheism.

Gayatri Mantra

Om bhūr bhuvaḥ svaḥ
tat-savitur vareṇyaṁ
bhargo devasya dhīmahi
dhiyo yo naḥ pracodayāt.

Om bhūr bhuvaḥ svaḥ
ohm bhoor bhu-vah svah

tat-savitur vareṇyaṁ
tut sah-vee-toor va-rayn-yum

bhargo devasya dhīmahi
bhahr-go day-vah-sya dhee-ma-hi

dhiyo yo naḥ pracodayāt
dhee-yo yo nah prah-cho-da-yaat

Translation:
We meditate on the divine light of Savitṛ (the Sun),
Who illuminates all realms: earth, atmosphere, and heavens.
May that divine brilliance inspire and guide our thoughts.

One of the most revered hymns from the Rig Veda, dedicated to Surya, the sun god.

The Hymn to Selene

You rise in silence, silver-veiled,
O Selene, Queen of the Night.

Draped in glowing mist, you ascend the sky,
Your white light flowing across the dark.
Calm-eyed guardian of the stars,
You move with grace among the heavens.

You see the secrets that sunlight hides,
The thoughts unspoken, the dreams not yet born.
Your radiance soothes the restless soul,
And guides the wanderer home.

O Selene, shining mother of stillness,
Grant me serenity in your cool light.
May my thoughts be clear as your reflection,
My heart steady as your arc.

Selene is the ancient Greek goddess of the Moon — a luminous, divine figure who drives her silver chariot across the night sky.

Incan Hymn to Mama Killa (Peru)

Silver mother, Lady of the night sky,
Your light touches the lakes and mountains.
Watch over us, your children,
and bring calm to the dark.

Mama Killa was the moon goddess and protector of women.
