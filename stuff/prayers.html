Prayers

Adapted for Ritual or Meditation

Morning

I give You thanks for You have mercifully restored my breathe (soul). Great is Your faith in me.

Evening

May it be Your will that I lie down in peace and that I arise in peace.

For the Sun

O Aten, Living Sun,
You are eternal, you are one.
The earth rejoices at your sight,
And I rejoice in your bright light.

For the Moon

Hear me, lovely Selene,
night's shining queen,
Joyful and all-seeing.

Come, blessed goddess,
bring peace and healing to us,
as you shine your gentle light
in the cool hours of the night.

For the Rain

O Adad, let the cool rain fall!
Let dark clouds spread across the sky.
May the river rise and crops grow tall,
May the land drink deep as the storms pass by.

Bedtime Shema

May it be Your will, Lord my God and God of my ancestors,
that I lie down in peace and that I arise in peace.

The Great Hymn to the Aten

You rise in beauty on the horizon of the sky,
O Living Aten, source of all life.

When you ascend in the eastern light,
You fill every land with your radiance.
Your brilliance reaches to the farthest corners —
Even the unknown lands receive your light.

Though you are high above,
Your rays touch the earth below.
Though your form is hidden,
Your presence is known through what you sustain.

The world is brought to life by your hand.
Plants grow, trees blossom, birds sing,
Their wings outstretched in praise of your spirit.

O Aten, Living Sun,
You are eternal, you are one.
The earth rejoices in your sight,
And I rejoice in your great light.

The earth rejoices in your light,
And I rejoice in you.

Aten was originally just the physical sun disk, but during the reign of Akhenaten, Aten was elevated to the one true god in a brief experiment with monotheism.

Gayatri Mantra

Om bhūr bhuvaḥ svaḥ
ohm bhoor bhoo-vah swa-ha

tat-savitur vareṇyaṁ
taht sah-vee-toor va-rayn-yum

bhargo devasya dhīmahi
bhahr-go day-vah-sya dhee-ma-hee

dhiyo yo naḥ pracodayāt
dhee-yo yo nah prah-cho-da-yaat

Translation:
We meditate on the divine light of Savitr (the Sun),
Who illuminates all realms: earth, atmosphere, and heavens.
May that divine brilliance inspire and guide our thoughts.

One of the most revered hymns from the Rig Veda, dedicated to Surya, the sun god.

Orphic Hymn to Selene

(Traditional Invocation, circa 2nd century CE)

Hear me, lovely Selene,
shining queen of the night,
all-seeing and joyful, light-bringer to mortals.

Bull-horned Moon, wandering quietly through the heavens,
swift-footed and beautiful,
Mother of months and timekeeper of the night.

Radiant in your golden crown,
you ride your long-maned horses across the heavens,
and in silence you gaze upon the sleeping Earth.

Lamp of the night,
gleaming guide through darkness,
nourisher of all growth,
friend to women and lovers,
companion of the mysteries.

Come, blessed goddess,
shine your gentle light upon us,
and bring peace and healing in the cool hours of the night.

Selene is the ancient Greek goddess of the Moon — a luminous, divine figure who drives her silver chariot across the night sky.

Incan Hymn to Mama Killa (Peru)

Silver mother, Lady of the night sky,
Your light touches the lakes and mountains.
Watch over us, your children,
and bring calm to the dark.

Mama Killa was the moon goddess and protector of women.

Sumerian Prayer to Adad

O Adad, bring forth the rain!
Bring forth the clouds from the sky.
May the river swell, may the crops grow tall,
May the land drink deeply and be fertile.

In Sumerian culture, Adad (or Ishkur) was the god of storms and rain.
