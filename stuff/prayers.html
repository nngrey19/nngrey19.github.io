Orphic Hymn 78: To Eos (Dawn)

Translation by Apostolos N. Athanassakis & Benjamin M. Wolkow

Hear me, O blessed Dawn, mother of the stars and dew,
rosy-armed and divine, brimming with noble light.

You come in your chariot drawn by holy steeds,
you drive through the sky with your light-bringing whip,

O maiden clothed in saffron, you beam out your light,
you carry the flame that gives men life.

Mortals and immortals alike cry out in joy at your rising,
as you shine on all things with your brilliant fire.

Ever youthful and fresh, you chase away the darkness,
you open the gates of the sky for the shining Sun.

Come, blessed goddess, bringer of light and day,
drive away evil dreams and keep sweet sleep away.

For the Moon

Orphic Hymn to Selene

Lucid and Pontiac

Splendid bringer of light,
bull-horned goddess Moon,
crossing the sky,
racing through night,
we honor you.

Stars attend your orbit
as you glide,
a torch in the darkness,
waxing and waning,
male and female.
We honor you.

Brooding amber
shining in the night,
all-seeing vigilance
in the sky of stars,
evening’s splendor,
like a jewel you offer
fulfillment and favor.

In your long cloak of shadow
moving in circles,
you shepherd the Milky Way.
Holy and gentle lady of the stars,
let your light shine
and save us.

The Great Hymn to the Aten

Translation by Miriam Lichtheim

You appear beautiful on the horizon of the sky,
O living Aten, Beginning of life!
When you rise on the eastern horizon,
You fill every land with your beauty.

You are beauteous, great, radiant, high over every land;
Your rays embrace the lands to the limit of all that you made.
Being Re, you reach their limits,
You bend them for your beloved son.

Though you are far, your rays are on earth;
Though seen by no one, your movements are known.

When you set on the western horizon,
The land is in darkness, in the manner of death.
Sleepers are in their chambers, heads covered,
One eye does not see the other.

All their goods are stolen, even though under their heads,
And they are unaware of it.
Every lion comes out from its den,
All the serpents bite;

Darkness hovers, earth is silent,
For he who made them rests on his horizon.

Earth brightens when you rise on the horizon,
When you shine as Aten by day.
You dispel the darkness,
You cast your rays, and the Two Lands are in festivity.

Awake they stand on their feet,
You have raised them up.
Their limbs cleansed, they don their clothing,
Their arms uplifted in praise at your rising.

The entire land performs its work:
All cattle are satisfied with their pasturage;
Trees and plants flourish,
The birds flutter from their nests,
Their wings greeting your Ka.

All flocks skip on their feet,
All that fly and alight live as you rise for them.

Ships fare north, fare south as well,
Every road is open at your rising.
Fish in the river leap before you,
Your rays are in the midst of the sea.

You make the seed in women,
You create the fluid in men,
You give life to the son in his mother's womb,
You soothe him that he may not weep,
You nurse him in the womb, giving breath to sustain all that you made.

When he comes forth from the womb to breathe
On the day of his birth,
You open his mouth completely,
You provide for his needs.

When the chick cries in the egg-shell,
You give him breath within it to keep him alive;
When you have made him fully, he breaks the shell
And comes forth from the egg to announce his completion,
Walking on his legs as he comes forth.

How manifold it is, what you have made!
They are hidden from sight,
O Sole God beside whom there is none!

You made the earth according to your desire,
While you were alone:
All men, herds, and flocks,
All upon earth that walk on legs,
All on high that fly with their wings,

The lands of Khor and Kush,
The land of Egypt—
You set every man in his place,
You supply their needs,
Everyone has his food,
His lifetime is counted.

Their tongues differ in speech,
Their characters likewise;
Their skins are distinct,
For you distinguished the peoples.

You made the Nile in the netherworld,
You bring it up at your desire to keep them alive,
For you made them for yourself:

O Lord of all of them, laboring for them,
O Aten of daytime, great in glory!

All distant lands, you make them live,
You have placed a Nile in the sky that it may fall for them,
Making floods on the mountains like the sea
To water their fields among their towns.

How excellent are your ways, O Lord of eternity!
A Nile in the sky for the foreign peoples
And for all the creatures of the desert that go on foot—
Yet the real Nile comes from the underworld for Egypt.

Your rays nurse all fields:
When you rise they live, they grow for you.
You make the seasons in order to rear all that you made:
The winter to cool them,
The heat that they may taste you.

You made the distant sky in order to rise in it,
In order to see all that you make.
While you were alone,
Rising in your form as the living Aten,
Appearing, shining, withdrawing or approaching,

You made millions of forms from yourself alone:
Cities, towns, fields, the river's course.
All eyes see you above them
As you are the Aten of the daytime on high.

You are in my heart,
There is no other who knows you
Except your son Neferkheperure, Sole-one-of-Re
Whom you have taught your ways and your might.

Those on earth come from your hand as you made them.
When you have dawned, they live.
When you set, they die.
You yourself are lifetime; one lives by you.

All eyes are on your beauty until you set.
All labor is put down when you rest in the west.

You establish the world, and raise up what you made.
You are in my heart, and there is no one else who knows you
But for your son, whom you have taught your ways.

He who rises in your name shall live forever and ever.

Aten was originally just the physical sun disk, but during the reign of Akhenaten, Aten was elevated to the one true god in a brief experiment with monotheism.

Gayatri Mantra

Om bhūr bhuvaḥ svaḥ
ohm bhoor bhoo-vah swa-ha

tat-savitur vareṇyaṁ
taht sah-vee-toor va-rayn-yum

bhargo devasya dhīmahi
bhahr-go day-vah-sya dhee-ma-hee

dhiyo yo naḥ pracodayāt
dhee-yo yo nah prah-cho-da-yaat

Translation:
We meditate on the divine light of Savitr (the Sun),
Who illuminates all realms: earth, atmosphere, and heavens.
May that divine brilliance inspire and guide our thoughts.

One of the most revered hymns from the Rig Veda, dedicated to Surya, the sun god.

Orphic Hymn to Selene

Athanassakis & Wolkow

Hear me, O divine queen,
O light-bringing and splendid Selene, O bull-horned Moon,
crossing the air as you race with night.
Nocturnal, torch-bearing, maiden of beautiful stars, O Moon,
waxing and waning,
feminine and masculine,
luminous, lover of horses,
mother of time, bearer of fruit,
amber-colored, moody,
shining in the night, all-seeing and vigilant,
surrounded by beautiful stars, you delight in the quiet
and in the richness of the night,
you grant fulfillment and favor
as, like a jewel, you shine in the night.

Long-cloaked marshal of the stars,
wise maiden whose motion is circular,
come, O blessed and gentle lady,
lady of the stars, through your own light
shine and save, O maiden,
your new initiates.

Selene is the ancient Greek goddess of the Moon — a luminous, divine figure who drives her silver chariot across the night sky.

Incan Hymn to Mama Killa (Peru)

Silver mother, Lady of the night sky,
Your light touches the lakes and mountains.
Watch over us, your children,
and bring calm to the dark.

Mama Killa was the moon goddess and protector of women.

Sumerian Prayer to Adad

O Adad, bring forth the rain!
Bring forth the clouds from the sky.
May the river swell, may the crops grow tall,
May the land drink deeply and be fertile.

In Sumerian culture, Adad (or Ishkur) was the god of storms and rain.
