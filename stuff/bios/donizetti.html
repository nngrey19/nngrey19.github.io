Donizetti

Gaetano Donizetti (November 29, 1797 – April 8, 1848) was an Italian composer, best known for his almost 70 operas. Along with Gioachino Rossini and Vincenzo Bellini, he was a leading composer of the bel canto opera style.

Nationality: Italian

Location: Known for working in Naples, Rome, Milan, Vienna, and Paris

Patrons: Wealthy aristocrats and royalties including Emperor Ferdinand I of Austria

Contemporaries: Vincenzo Bellini, Gioachino Rossini, Giuseppe Verdi

Accomplishments:

  • Wrote over 75 operas
  • Helped popularize opera (semiseria opera semiseria contains elements of comedy but also of pathos)
  • Known for his melodic and lyrical style

Best Known Works:

  • L'elisir d'amore
  • Lucia di Lammermoor
  • La Fille du Régiment
  • Don Pasquale
  • Maria Stuarda
