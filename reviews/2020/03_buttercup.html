Creeping Buttercup

June 1, 2020

Today's flower is the Creeping Buttercup. We observed it in several spots along the forest branch of the MAC Trail during our midday walk. It is not a showy flower but it is prolific. I suspect it can be found throughout the park and city.

After a little research, I learned that the flower is native to Europe, Asia and northwestern Africa and is generally viewed as invasive in the U.S. It is a stoloniferous plant, meaning it has stolons or runners, which are stems that grow close the ground and form roots.
