Neolithic Revolution

December 26, 2019

Study Notes: These notes are largely cribbed from other sources. Most sources are referenced at the bottom of the page.

The Neolithic Revolution (or Agricultural Revolution) refers to the transition from a lifestyle of hunting and gathering to one of agriculture and settlement, beginning initially in the Fertile Crescent (Ancient Egypt, Mesopotamia) around 10,000 to 8,000 BC.

Early cities (or proto-cities) appeared at Jericho and Catal Huyuk around 6000 BCE. The first "civilizations" emerged in Lower Mesopotamia (Sumer) and Egypt around 3000 BCE. They were followed by the Harappan civilization in the Indus River Valley (2500 BCE) and Chinese civilization along the Yellow and Yangtze Rivers (2200 BCE).

The transition from proto-cities to civilizations, while still disputed, may be associated with the development of state structures and hierarchies. Writing, which facilitated the administration of cities, is another important feature.
