Ulysses, John Adams, Dvořák

June 24, 2022

I finally "finished" Ulysses. I could not bring myself to read it word for word. I will leave that for Joyce fanatics and masochists. My process was more like skim-reading with a review of various online summaries. I have no idea why this is a towering work of modernist fiction. It feels more like a series of gimmicks - one for each chapter. Do the gimmicks add anything important? I could not tell. I am glad I finally gave the book a shot and am even more glad that it is now in the rearview mirror.

The latest episode of This Classical Life featured Maya Youssef. Youssef plays the qanun, 78-stringed Middle Eastern plucked zither. That in itself is intriguing. Here is a sample of her music.

Although I am a big fan of the minimalism of Philip Glass, I don't know much about other minimalists, like John Adams. Shaking and Trembling from Shaker Loops was a nice introduction to his work.
