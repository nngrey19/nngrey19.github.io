Timeline Art

June 23, 2022
1000 – 1150 Romanesque
  • large-scale architectural style, emulating Classical Roman styles
  • sturdy and solid; thick walls, smaller windows, columns
  • Moissac Abbey, France, 1060
1140 – 1600 Gothic
  • architectural style originating in France
  • larger and taller buildings, flying buttresses, ribbed vaults, pointed arches
  • more windows and light, rose windows, stained glass
  • Abbey of Saint-Denis, ca 1135 – 44
  • Chartres Cathedral, 1194 - 1220
1400 - 1600 Renaissance
  • Naturalism, precision of human anatomy, contrapposto, chiaroscuro, linear perspective
  • artists drew inspiration from the Classical era
Early Renaissance
  • Cimabué, c. 1240 – 1302, Santa Croce Crucifixion
  • Giotto, c. 1267 – 1337, Lamentation
  • Brunelleschi, Donatello, Masacio
High Renaissance
  • Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, Raphael
Northern Renaissance
  • Jan van Eyck, Albrecht Dürer
Mannerism (Late Renaissance), 1520 - 1600
  • expressive and exaggerated, elongated limbs
  • Jacopo da Pontormo, 1494 – 1557, Visitation of Carmignano, 1528
  • El Greco, 1541 – 1614, La Santísima Trinidad (‘The Holy Trinity’), 1577-1579
1600 - 1750 Baroque
  • drama, tension
  • religious and biblical narratives
  • chiaroscuro, tenebrism
  • naturalism, realism
  • Caravaggio, 1571 – 1610, The Calling of Saint Matthew, 1599-1600
  • Artemisia Gentileschi, 1593 – 1656, Judith Beheading Holofernes, 1611-1612
  • Bernini, 1598 – 1680, Apollo and Daphne, 1622-1625
  • Peter Paul Rubens, 1577 – 1640, Flemish The Three Graces, ca 1635
  • Velázquez, 1599 – 1660, Spanish Las Meninas (‘The Maids of Honour’), 1656 - 1657
  • Rembrandt, 1606 – 1669, Dutch The Night Watch, 1642
1720 – 1760 Rococo
  • luxurious, extravagant, and light-hearted
  • lighter color palette than Baroque
  • curving forms, asymmetrical values, nature-inspired motifs
  • Jean-Honore Fragonard, The Swing, 1767
  • Antoine Watteau, Francois Boucher
1750 - 1850 Neoclassicism
  • Renewed interest in classical antiquity, harmony, simplicity, and proportion
  • movement away from the overly decorative style of the Baroque and Rococo, “Noble simplicity”
  • didactic subject matter
  • Nicolas Poussin, Anton Raphael Mengs, Benjamin West, Angelica Kauffman
  • Jacques-Louis David, 1748 – 1825, Oath of the Horatii, 1786
  • Jean-Auguste-Dominique Ingres 1780 – 1867, La Grande Odalisque, 1814
1800 - 1850 Romanticism
  • Imaginative elements, focus on passion, emotion, and observing the senses
  • nationalism, personal and subjective, and concerns with justice and equality
  • the sublime and the natural world
  • Théodore Géricault, Liberty Leading the People, 1830
  • Eugène Delacroix, The Raft of the Medusa, 1818 – 1819
  • Francisco Goya, Third of May 1808, 1814
  • Henry Fuseli, The Nightmare, 1781
1848 - 1900 Realism
  • reaction to social changes caused by the Industrial Revolution
  • Honoré Daumier, 1808 – 1879, Third-Class Carriage, 1862
  • Jean-François Millet, 1814 – 1875, The Gleaners, 1857
  • Gustave Courbet, 1819 – 1877, The Stone Breakers, 1849
1848 – 1854 Pre-Raphaelite
Impressionism 1870 – 1900
Naturalism 1880 – 1900
Post-Impressionism 1885–1910
Fauvism 1900–1935
Expressionism 1905–1920
Art Noveau 1895 – 1915
Cubism 1907–1914
Surrealism 1917–1950
Abstract Expressionism 1940–1950s
