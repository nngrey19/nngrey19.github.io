Reginaldo degli Scrovegni

January, 9 2023

Reginaldo degli Scrovegni was a 14th money-lender or usurer, which was considered a mortal sin at the time. Dante placed Reginaldo in the Seventh Circle of Hell. Reginaldo's son, Enrico, was also a money-lender. Enrico was so concerned about the spiritual ramifications of his vocation that he built the Scrovegni Chapel in order to reclaim his soul.

Enrico commissioned Giotto to paint the frescos around the interior of the Scrovegni Chapel. Giotto's work, which is more naturalistic than most art of the period, is seen as a harbinger of the Renaissance.
