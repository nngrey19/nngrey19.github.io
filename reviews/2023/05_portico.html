Portico

March 25, 2023

A portico is a covered porch, often with columns. A colonnade is a long sequence of columns. An entablature is a horizontal decorative band resting on top of a series of columns. Entablatures are commonly divided into the architrave, the frieze, and the cornice. A capital is the topmost section of a column, which helps to carry the load. Styles of capitals include doric, ionic and corinthian.

zodiac

It is Wagner week. I've been watching a 2016 performance of Tristan und Isolde on Met on Demand and reading the chapter on Wagner in Schonberg's The Lives of the Great Composers. In that chapter, Schonberg describes Nietzsche's admiration for Wagner, which got me listening to the episodes on Nietzsche in the Philosophize This podcast.

The first of these episodes references a variety ofTrue World Theories, that people subscribe to in order to answer the question "what is the meaning of life?" These theories hold that there is an imperfect material world in which we live and a perfect "true" world (heaven, the forms) that we can aspire to.

The second episode described the concept of "Will to Power", which is described as realizing one true potential or self-expression. It is why we do things above and beyond self-preservation. Actually, Nietzsche never gives a detailed explanation of the term and people have been trying to interpret what he meant ever since.

I found myself thinking about identity and how we actively shape our sense of self - I am an artist, I am an intellectual, I am a country boy. I wonder if the will to power is akin to how we strive to inhabit our identities.
