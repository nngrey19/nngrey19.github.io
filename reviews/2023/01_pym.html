General Events, Recent Readings

January, 1 2023

I finished Barbara Pym's A Glass of Blessings. Lovely, restrained, engaging. My mother loved Pym's books and now I know why. I also have Penelope Lively on my radar.

I just started LeCarre's Call for the Dead, the first in the George Smiley series. I had been waiting to get through his other novels before starting the Smiley series, but honestly, I had lost track of where I was, and after finishing A Glass of Blessings, I was desperate for something more to read. None of my other library holds were ready.

Carl Marie von Weber week has concluded, and now it is time for Hector Berlioz. I am vaguely dreading the Romantics. I fear the sturm and drang may be too much for me, but we will see.

This weekend, I bought some sewing material. Jim will be giving me my first lesson (making an apron) later in the the month.
