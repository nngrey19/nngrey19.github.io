What is the difference between tenebrism and chiaroscuro? Tenebrism, or dramatic illumination, is used to achieve a spotlight effect. Some areas of the painting are completely black while others are illuminated, usually from a single source of light. Chiaroscuro (kee-AR-É™-SKOOR-oh) is a (less extreme) contrast of light and dark used to create the illusion of three-dimensional form.

The impact of the reformation and counter-reformation on art.

The Reformation began in 1517 when Martin Luther posted his Ninety-five Theses. Over time, it lead to the creation of the two main branches of Protestantism - the Evangelical Lutheran churches, which followed the teachings of Luther, and the Reformed Churches, which followed the ideas of John Calvin and Huldrych Zwingli.

The Council of Trent, 1545 - 1563, was a council of the Catholic Church prompted by the Protestant Reformation. It has been described as the embodiment of the Counter-Reformation.

The Reformed Church removed religious imagery, while the Lutheran church and the Council of Trent offered guidance on what type of imagery was appropiate.
