Tenebrism

March 15, 2023

What is the difference between tenebrism and chiaroscuro? Tenebrism, or dramatic illumination, is used to achieve a spotlight effect. Some areas of the painting are completely black while others are illuminated, usually from a single source of light. Chiaroscuro (kee-AR-ə-SKOOR-oh) is a (less extreme) contrast of light and dark used to create the illusion of three-dimensional form.

The impact of the reformation and counter-reformation on art.

The Reformation began in 1517 when Martin Luther posted his Ninety-five Theses. Over time, it lead to the creation of the two main branches of Protestantism – the Evangelical Lutheran churches, which followed the teachings of Luther, and the Reformed Churches, which followed the teachings of John Calvin and Huldrych Zwingli.

The Catholic Church responded to the Protestant Reformation with The Council of Trent, 1545 - 1563, which has been described as the embodiment of the Counter-Reformation.

The Reformed Church removed religious imagery, while the Lutheran church and the Council of Trent provided guidance on what type of imagery was appropiate.
