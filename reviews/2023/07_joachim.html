Joseph Joachim

April 15, 2023

Joseph Joachim (1831 - 1907) (pronounced Yo-Aah-Chim) was a Hungarian-German violinist and close friend of Brahms. He was also one of the first performers to record his music. Here he plays Brahms Hungarian Dance #1.

Coincidentally, Ryan likes the work of Joachim Neugroschel, a translator of Kafka and others.

Melisma – a passage of multiple notes sung to one syllable of text, as in Gregorian chant.

Max Richter, composer and pianist. He had a minimalist period - e.g., On the Nature of Daylight. He also interpreted The Four Seasons.

Isabella Leonarda, 1620 – 1704, Italian composer. Her sonata's are among her most popular works, e.g., no. 10. I listened to an episode of Composer of the Week on Hildegard of Bingen and Leonarda.
