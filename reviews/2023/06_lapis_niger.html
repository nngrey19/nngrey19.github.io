Lapis Niger

April 2, 2023

The Lapis Niger ("Black Stone") is an ancient shrine in the Roman Forum. The black marble paving (1st century BC) of the Lapis Niger overlie an ancient altar and a stone block with one of the earliest known Old Latin inscriptions (c. 570–550 BC).

The Battle of Corinth in 146 BC marked the end of the Achaean War and the beginning of the period of Roman domination in Greek history.

In "The Beginnings of Roman Literature" (episode 42), Doug Metz notes that there was a concentration of literary activity in Rome from 100 BC to 100 AD.
