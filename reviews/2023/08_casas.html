April 15, 2023

Above two noteworthy paintings from the National Gallery's exhibit "Inventing Modern Art."

We went to the Portland Baroque Orchestra's final concert of the season, A Mozart Jubilee. The program included the Sinfonia Concertante in F Major for Two Violins and Viola, Op. 10/1 by Joseph Bologne, a French Creole, virtuoso violinist and composer, and a free man of color conducting leading orchestras in Paris.

ETA (Ernst Theodor Amadeus) Hoffmann, 1776 – 1822, German Romantic author of fantasy and Gothic horror. His stories form the basis of Jacques Offenbach's opera The Tales of Hoffmann, in which Hoffmann appears (heavily fictionalized) as the hero.

aria vs recitative

Max Richter, composer and pianist. He had a minimalist period - e.g., On the Nature of Daylight. He also interpreted The Four Seasons.

Isabella Leonarda, 1620 – 1704, Italian composer. Her sonata's are among her most popular works, e.g., no. 10. I listened to an episode of Composer of the Week on Hildegard of Bingen and Leonarda.

This week I read about Hugo Wolf, who is best known for his leider. I've also started the chapter on Strauss, Offenbach, and Sullivan in The Lives of the Great Composers and found episodes of Composer of the Week on each of them.

I finished The Furrows by Namwali Serpell. It started strong but descended into confusion, or maybe it was some sort of literary technique – like dream-state – that I simply could not follow.

I started Egypt's Golden Couple by John and Colleen Darnell and I, Claudius by Robert Graves. I read I, Claudius, or an excerpt of it, when I was a young man and recall liking it. I hope it is as enjoyable this time around.
