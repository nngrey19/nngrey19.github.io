Holy Roman Empire

The Persian Empire, also known as the Achaemenid Empire, was founded by Cyrus the Great in 550 BC and lasted until its conquest by Alexander the Great in 330 BC.

The Seleucid Empire was a Hellenistic state that existed from 312 BCE to 63 BCE. It was founded by Seleucus I Nicator, a former general of Alexander the Great, after Alexander's death.

The Parthian Empire was founded in 247 BC by the Arsacid dynasty, a group of Iranian rulers who rebelled against the Seleucid Empire. The Parthians were a separate and distinct dynasty from the Achaemenids who ruled the Persian Empire.

However, the Parthians did draw on some of the cultural and administrative traditions of the Achaemenid Empire. For example, they adopted some of the Achaemenid administrative practices, such as the use of satraps (provincial governors) to govern their territories.

The Parthian Empire was established in the same region as the Persian Empire, and the Parthians considered themselves the rightful heirs to the legacy of Iranian culture and civilization that had been established by the Achaemenids.

The Sassanid Empire was an ancient Persian empire that existed from 224 CE to 651 CE. It was founded by Ardashir I, who overthrew the Parthian Empire and established himself as the new king of Persia.

The Arab Muslim conquests in the 7th century CE were a series of military campaigns that were carried out by Arab Muslim armies against the Byzantine Empire, Sassanid Empire, and other neighboring states.

The Sassanid Empire ultimately fell to the Arab Muslim conquests in the 7th century CE, which marked the beginning of the Islamic Golden Age and the spread of Islam throughout the region.
